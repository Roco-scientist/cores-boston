Update: BMC Plan Terminated by the State

The fight for Franklin Park is entering a new chapter.

The state has stopped the previous proposal. Now, we must ensure the future of this historic green space is guided by Environmental Justice and a fully community-led process.

Moving Forward Together

CORES is a citizen-formed coalition of residents who live near and rely on Franklin Park. We successfully fought for transparency, leading the state to terminate the previous plans that lacked community input.

Our work doesn't stop here. Franklin Park was designed to be a sanctuary for the health and wellness of all Boston residents. Any future redevelopment must honor this original intent and be driven directly by the communities most impacted by these decisions.

Our Demand:

Nothing about us, without us. We demand the city and state commit to a transparent planning process that centers the voices of surrounding neighborhoods and adheres strictly to Environmental Justice standards.

Our Guiding Principles

Environmental Justice

We believe marginalized communities deserve equal protection from environmental burdens and equitable access to vital green spaces. Franklin Park must remain an ecological asset, not an afterthought.

Community-Led Design

Top-down development has failed our neighborhoods. Future plans must start with grass-roots community visioning, ensuring residents have the final say in what is built in their backyard.

Health & Wellness

We support honoring Frederick Law Olmsted’s original vision: preserving the park as a refuge for physical and mental health, recreation, and community connection for all Bostonians.
