Advocating for a
Balanced Franklin Park.

Help us ensure that future redevelopment of the Shattuck site is guided by regional equity, community vision, and Environmental Justice.

The Collective Message

To: Governor Healey, Mayor Wu, and Secretary Walsh

As a resident and stakeholder in the communities surrounding Franklin Park, I am writing to urge a more balanced and equitable approach to the redevelopment of the Shattuck Hospital site.

We recognize the importance of clinical and social services, but Environmental Justice requires that these services be distributed fairly across the region. Currently, the Franklin Park Corridor carries nearly five times the density of SUD supportive housing compared to the rest of the city. We believe that true equity means no single neighborhood is over-saturated, ensuring the park remains a balanced sanctuary for all.

What is the Franklin Park Corridor?

The "Corridor" is a data-defined region comprising the ten wards immediately surrounding the park (Wards 8, 9, 11–17, and 19). While these neighborhoods are home to 74% non-white residents, they host a vastly disproportionate 5.28 SUD housing sites per 10k residents, compared to just 1.16 in the rest of Boston.

I stand with CORES in advocating for:

  • Community-Led Visioning: A process that centers the voices of local residents to determine the mix of uses that best serves the neighborhood's needs.
  • Geographic Equity: A regional strategy that partners with all municipalities to provide recovery services, rather than concentrating them in one demographic corridor.
  • Park Restoration: Prioritizing Frederick Law Olmsted’s vision of the park as a vital regional asset for public health, recreation, and open space.

Nothing about us, without us. I look forward to working together toward a planning process that respects our community's needs and protects our historic green space.
